What is Sinaesthetic?

Sinaesthetic is a draw-by-sound web application. A sound recognition system takes user input, parses it and controls the application based on the recognised sounds. Take a look at the tutorial (currently available only for the Chrome browser) to get a glimpse of the Sinaesthetic prototype:

...or check out a protoype of the UI (opens in a new tab).

Application interaction

The user can interact through the application by sound or mouse. Sound input is received by voice and the feedback is provided through app recorded sounds and text to speech.

Application concepts

Voice driven drawing: The application provides voice controls for drawing. Besides the usual editing commands like save, empty and restore, voice controls include selecting a colour, a paint tool, positioning it on the canvas drawing.

Painting to sound: The application is able to replay a painting through sound, similar to a sonar. The painting is transformed from a two dimensional pixel map to a linear sound stream in which each pixel is transformed to a sound. This feature will also aid accessibility for visual impaired persons.

Tutorials The application provides interactive tutorials through which users can learn the application controls.

Project Identity

Colour scheme

Justification of the chromatic choice for the visual identity of the application: the chromatic combination between blue and orange is designed to be aesthetically pleasant and to emphasize the characteristics of both colors, given the contrasting nature of this combination. These two colors are usually used in professional circumstances for two age categories: young and middle-age, they have in common the tendency to both concentrate and relax. It is scientifically proven that, while orange accesses the left hemisphere of the brain, blue accesses the right one. Combining the two sets of capacities and functions of the human brain usually helps to visually concentrate to stimuli, regardless of their nature. In other words, whenever this chromatic combination is used, the subject is going to improve his performance of the cognitive processes of both intellectual and artistic brain functions. There are no further suggestions regarding the gender of the target that shold be taken into consideration, reason for which this combination is suitable for both men and women equally.

Concerning the inner given meaning of each of these colors by experts in visual arts, blue is generally regarded as a great stimulus for patience, peace and calmness, while orange represents friendliness and trustworthiness of the given environment, while it also covers the energy boost necessary to keeping focused. In combination, these two colors tend to help the subject keep focused for a longer period of time. Since it's scientifically proven that the human brain cannot remain focused for more than 20 minutes continuously, unless there are exceptional conditions, this chromatic combination is proven to have kept the subjects focused for more than half an hour in an unfriendly and distractive environment.

Naming

Synesthezia is the cognitive capacity of the brain to mistaken a stimulus of a kind after having received it and decoding it in another stimulus' manner. For instance, a visual stimulus can be perceived by a subject like a sound or like a state of mind. This kind of misinterpretation of the human brain is today believed to be a cognitive dysfunction. However, history shows, this kind of twisted cognitive perception has helped enrich the artistic perception of great personalities, by managing to combine two different perceptions and obtain another given feeling from their blending. In the context of this application, synesthezia is used to metaphorically represent the two different kinds of stimuli that are used, interpreted and transformed. Another meaning of the first part in the naming of this application (explaining the use of 'i' instead of 'y') is based on the sinus-cosine relationship and the complementarity between these two - similarly, the two distinct senses are being transformed into a complementary result of their combination. Aesthetically speaking, the capacity to transform an acoustic stimulus into a visual representation isn't only based on a common act of semiosis, but also implies that there is a converging result of artistic nature, meaning that both semiotically speaking and aesthetically at the same time, the application in itself stands for a senzorial improvement. Concerning the semiosis, the act of decodification and representation of a stimulus towards another one, the complexity of the reality that is represented through the initial stimulus increases proportionally with its corespondent representation, making it not only useful but also aesthetically pleasant. The sinus and cosinus are perceived by the human brain in an interdependent relation of complementarity, and so are the complementary colors used in the visual identity of the application (blue and orange), suggesting the semiotic complementarity of the senses that are being involved in the codification- decodification-recodification-representation process implied, thus creating a visual identitary unity.

Conclusion: Popular drawing software such as Microsoft Paint or Adobe Photoshop usually have the most used tools (pen, color selector etc.) in a toolbar on the left and some general tools (save, undo etc.) in a menu on top. This separation helps logically group tools and keeps the user in a familiar environment. However, the top menu is a main trait of desktop applications and the number of tools offered does not require cascading menu grouping. In order to save screen space and center the canvas for good focus, placing the drawing tools on the left to preserve familiarity and the general, utility tools on the right, seems like the best solution.

Q2: Type of sounds recognized?
O: Musical notes
O: Words
C: Intuitive
C: Language independent
C: High number of possible commands
C: Easy to use

Conclusion: While drawing by playing music is language independent and can create a high number of possible commands, using actual words to draw is more intuitive and easier to use while also not restricting the number of possible commands. Also adding the fact that it can be implemented easier and more reliable using voice recognition engines, drawing by using words seems to be the best solution.

Q3: How to draw using words?
O: Instruction based ("move left 3 units", "brush color red" etc.)
O: Based on the physical characteristic of the sound (pitch, length etc.)
C: Easy to use
C: Intuitive
C: High accuracy

Conclusion: Using exact instructions offers a much higher accuracy and ease of use and seems to be the best solution.

User guide and demos

Sinaesthetic is an app you can use to draw using your mouse or your voice. You can save your work and come back to it, change colours, erase or identify a drawing using the sonar system. You can try Sinaesthetic yourself now: live demo.

Left tool box

Each icon has two modes: blue - deactivated, orange - activated. Here are the icons, from top to bottom, explained:

  • Pen: select this to enter drawing mode. More options will appear in the right tool box.
  • Microphone: When this button is active, the speech system is listening to commands.
  • Sound: When this button is active, the application is sending sounds (speech, sonar).
  • Play: When this button is used, the sonar is activated. It remains activated while the sonar is in use.
  • Save: When this button is pressed, your work is saved (you can view saved states in the right tool box by pressing the load button). Active while the canvas is being saved.
  • Load: Use this button to load a saved state from the persistence menu. Active when the persistence menu is open.
  • Clear: Use this button to erase all drawings from canvas.
  • Tutorials: This opens up some tutorials and info about how to use Sinaesthetic. Active when in tutorial mode.

Right tool box

This tool bar is used in drawing mode to choose colours or brush size and form. It is structured in three sections: top section contains the current selection of colour and brush, mid section contains the available brushes, third section contains the available colours. When a brush / colour is pressed (or requested via voice control), the top section is updated.

  • When selecting the pen, you can view and select thickness and colour.
  • When selecting the load button, you can view the persistence menu.

Persistence menu: When load is pressed (or requested via voice control), the persistence menu opens on the right. The persistence menu shows a preview of the previously saved drawings. When a preview is clicked the saved work is reloaded on the canvas.

Voice commands

  • Go: Activates the drawing menu, puts the brush on the center of the canvas.
  • Up, down, left, right: Moves the brush over the canvas.
  • Stroke: Presses / releases the brush on the canvas. Each brush move when the brush is pressed will draw on the canvas. Say once to press, say again to release.
  • Small round, Big round, Small square, Big square: Change the brush type.
  • Colours: A set of predefined colors can be named by the user to change the brush color.
  • Clear: Clears the canvas.
  • Save: Save the canvas.
  • Load: Opens the persistence menu. Shows a preview of the previous work, and allows user to chose to load an item from history.
  • First, Second, Third, Forth, Fifth: Load a painting from the storage, ordered by age, with the oldest being first.
  • Play: Brings up the sonar, which will replay the current drawing to the user.
